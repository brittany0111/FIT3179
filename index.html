Malaysia's Forestry Scene

Percentage of Forest Reserves Across Malaysian States in 2021

By 2021, Malaysia's forest reserves were heavily concentrated in Sabah, Perak, and Pahang, In contrast, urban centers like Kuala Lumpur and coastal regions such as Penang show little forest cover. This contrast highlights not only where nature thrives, but also where urgent conservation are needed to protect these critical ecosystems.

Which states are protecting the highest percentage of their land?

Sabah and Perak stand out as strong guardians of their forests, consistently dedicating large portions of their land to conservation. Meanwhile, other states show shifting trends. For example, Kedah improved their protection levels in 2013, whereas Pahang lost ground as development expands in 2009. This changing landscape tells a story not just of policy and land use, but of how each state weighs the balance between progress and preservation.

The Forces Behind the Falling Trees

Malaysia's forests have long felt the weight of human ambition, with permanent agriculture and logging leading the charge. Vast stretches of land once rich in biodiversity have been cleared for oil palm and rubber plantations, while logging steadily erodes what remains. These forces reveal a story of growth and loss, where economic progress has often come at nature's expense. Yet, by recognizing these drivers, Malaysia can begin to steer toward a future where forests and development grow side by side.
