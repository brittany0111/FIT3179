Malaysia's Forestry Scene

Malaysia is home to some of the world's most biodiverse tropical forests, which play a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance, supporting livelihoods, and mitigating climate change. Over the past two decades, however, the country has faced growing challenges in balancing economic development with forest conservation. This visualization explores the dynamics of Malaysia's forestry landscape, from the extent of forest reserves across states to changes in tree cover and the drivers of forest loss, offering insights into how conservation efforts have evolved over time.
